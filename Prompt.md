# Customer Cohort Intelligence Report — MethodCRM

> You are a senior business analyst, product strategist, and customer research expert specializing in B2B SaaS for SMBs.
>
> Your task is to produce a **decision-ready Customer Cohort Intelligence Report** for MethodCRM.
>
> Analyze the customer base by meaningful cohorts (e.g., industry, business model, size, maturity, usage patterns, QuickBooks configuration, or other relevant segmentation). For each cohort, synthesize all available data and infer missing context using domain knowledge of SMB operations.
>
> **Focus on generating insights that Product Marketing, Sales, Product, and Leadership can act on immediately.**

---

## STEP 1 — Define Cohorts

Identify the most strategically meaningful customer cohorts based on:

- Industry (e.g., field services, distribution, professional services, manufacturing)
- Company size (revenue, employees)
- Operational complexity
- Growth stage
- Technology maturity
- MethodCRM usage patterns
- QuickBooks edition/configuration
- Deployment characteristics (field vs office heavy)
- Any other high-impact segmentation

**Explain why each cohort matters to Method's business.**

---

## STEP 2 — Cohort Deep Dive *(Repeat for Each Cohort)*

### 🏢 Business Profile

- What does this business actually do?
- How do they make money?
- What does a typical company look like?
- How do they compete in their market?

### 🧑‍💼 Decision & Buying Dynamics

- Who are the key stakeholders? (economic buyer, technical buyer, champion, users)
- What triggers them to evaluate solutions?
- What objections and risks matter most?
- How do they make purchasing decisions?
- What alternatives do they consider?

### 🛠️ Operations & Day-in-the-Life

- Core workflows and processes
- Where work happens (field, warehouse, office)
- Critical handoffs between teams
- Bottlenecks, breakdowns, and workarounds
- Sources of errors or inefficiency

### 💻 Systems & Technology Stack

- Core systems in use (QuickBooks, spreadsheets, vertical tools, etc.)
- System of record vs shadow tools
- Integration realities
- Data reliability issues
- Automation maturity

### ⚠️ Pain Points & Risks

Rank by severity and urgency:

- Operational pain
- Financial risk
- Growth blockers
- Customer experience issues
- Compliance risks
- Scaling challenges

**Identify which pains are "must solve now" vs "nice to fix."**

### 💰 Economics & ROI Potential

- Where Method creates financial value
- Cost savings, revenue gains, risk reduction
- Labor reduction opportunities
- Error prevention
- Payback period expectations
- Metrics executives care about

### ⭐ How They Use MethodCRM

- Primary use cases
- Most valuable features
- Typical customization patterns
- Adoption level across roles
- What success looks like
- Common underutilization

### 👥 Users & Roles

- Who uses Method daily vs occasionally?
- Responsibilities of each role
- Technical skill level
- Mobility needs (desktop vs mobile)
- Training constraints

### 🥊 Competitive Landscape

- Current alternatives (software or manual)
- Why competitors win or lose
- Switching costs
- Vendor lock-in factors
- Method's true differentiators for this cohort

### 🚀 Growth & Strategic Direction

- How these businesses scale
- What breaks as they grow
- Future needs Method could serve
- Expansion opportunities (upsell/cross-sell)

### ❤️ Motivations & Priorities

- What leaders care about most
- Risk tolerance vs innovation appetite
- Emotional drivers (control, visibility, professionalism, growth)
- Success definitions

### 📣 Marketing & Reachability

- Where these customers seek information
- Influencers and advisors (accountants, consultants, peers)
- Channels that actually reach them
- Messaging that resonates
- Language they use to describe problems

### 🔁 Feedback & Retention Signals

- Indicators of satisfaction vs churn risk
- Adoption patterns that predict success
- Common complaints
- Expansion triggers
- Advocacy potential

---

## STEP 3 — Cross-Cohort Insights

Identify patterns across cohorts:

- Which cohorts generate the most revenue or growth?
- Which are easiest vs hardest to serve?
- Which have the strongest product-market fit?
- Which represent strategic expansion opportunities?
- Where Method is over- or under-positioned
- Shared pain themes across industries
- Major unmet needs

---

## STEP 4 — Strategic Recommendations

Provide actionable guidance for:

### Product Marketing

- Positioning by cohort
- Messaging angles
- Persona priorities
- Differentiation strategies
- Case study targets

### Sales

- Qualification signals
- Discovery questions
- Objection handling themes
- Value propositions by cohort
- Upsell opportunities

### Product

- Feature gaps with highest impact
- Opportunities for vertical solutions
- Integration priorities
- Usability improvements

### Customer Success

- Onboarding risks
- Adoption strategies
- Retention levers
- Expansion triggers

## STEP 5 — Executive Summary

Provide a concise summary answering:

- Who MethodCRM serves best today
- Where the biggest growth opportunities lie
- Where product or messaging is misaligned
- What leadership should prioritize

---

## Output Requirements

- **Be concrete, not generic**
- Use evidence where available and informed inference otherwise
- Highlight uncertainties explicitly
- Focus on actionable insight, not description
- Write in a professional, decision-ready tone
